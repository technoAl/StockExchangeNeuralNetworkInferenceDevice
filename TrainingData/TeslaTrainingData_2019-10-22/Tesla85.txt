The automotive industry is in the midst of a transformation as automakers shift strategies to innovate and incorporate electric vehicles (EVs) into their product lineups.

Globally, the auto industry is set to commit at least $300 billion over the next 5-10 years to bring EVs to market, and automakers are handling the industry shift in different ways. Here's how three major automakers stacked up in their Q3 2019 earnings, and what it says about their EV plans:As legacy automakers ramp up EV production, they could struggle to meet production targets, but for different reasons than Tesla. Tesla fell short of expected delivery numbers in Q3 2019, delivering 97,000 of the 100,000 vehicles that CEO Elon Musk had hinted at.Tesla has previously had issues hitting production numbers, engaging in end-of-quarter sprints and slogging through what Musk termed "production hell." However, legacy automakers could be challenged in other areas, as they must navigate complex labor dynamics in their transition to EV production.
