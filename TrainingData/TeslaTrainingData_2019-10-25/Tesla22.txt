A US federal court finds suspicionless searches of phones at the border is illegalA federal court has ruled that the government is not allowed to search travelers’ phones or other electronic devices at the U.S. border without first having reasonable suspicion of a crime. The case was brought by 11 travelers — 10 of whom are U.S. citizens — with support from the American Civil Liberties Union and the Electronic Frontier Foundation.4. The company has gone from hundreds of loads per week in 2016 to tens of thousands per week across the U.S.5.
